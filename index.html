Elementix

Elementrix is a card game designed to turn the confusing and complicated world of chemistry into a strategic battle of wits. Instead of just memorizing the periodic table from a textbook, players use a deck of cards to actually build chemical compounds and trigger reactions.

The game features three main types of cards: Element cards representing different atoms, reaction cards that change the environment (like adding heat, catalyst etc), and many more. By playing these cards into a shared "Reaction Zone," students learn through trial and error how different elements bond together.

The heart of the game is all about balance. To win points, a player must combine elements until their electrical charges (a.k.a valency) add up to zero, creating a stable molecule. No luck, pure knowledge. This makes the abstract idea of chemical bonding feel like a puzzle.

Elementrix helps students understand why chemicals react the way they do, rewarding those who can balance their equations and think like a scientist to earn the highest "stability score."

Why Elementrix ?

Students are first introduced to the concept of atoms and how they stick together. The game simplifies valency,being a perfect match as well for the pre-algebra skills typically mastered in 7th or 8th grade.
